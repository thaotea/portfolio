PROBLEM STATEMENT

After a pet strays from home, protocol includes contacting shelters, searching, and advertising both physically and online. Often, it's time consuming to search and post on multiple different websites and social platforms to be eventually lost in a sea of posts. Trace was created to gather the database of lost pets and encompass it onto an interactive map. This allows pet owners to see immediate updates on if their pet was seen as well as keep people alert for lost pets in their area.

IDEATION

During the ideation phase, our team wanted to empathize with the users of our app. In order to do so, we brainstormed ideas on what features would be most applicable in a situation where someone has lost their pets. From our initial research, we discovered similar apps like Rover that utilize facial recognition from photos to help reunite owners with their lost pets. This allowed for an efficient search of found pets within the database. We wanted to take a similar approach by having a centralized online database that users can filter and search through. To contrast having an endless list to scroll through, we wanted to implement an interactive map interface. We believed that time is an important matter in this situation and with a map interface users can be immediately noticed if a dog were to get loose or receive notifications on nearby lost pets.

STORYBOARD #1

We created storyboards in order to illustrate user scenarios. The first storyboard was created before we narrowed down our scope from missing people and pets to specifically missing pets. The storyboard depicts a mother who's daughter goes missing. She immediately contacts the police, but is still constantly worried about where her daughter is. She utilizes the Trace app to see updates people are sharing about the where abouts of her daughter. Through the updates, the police is able to reunite the mother and daughter. This storyboard depicts real-time updates that people can post during an crucial time.

STORYBOARD #2

The second storyboard depicts a nurse that works at a local animal shelter. Often many dogs that are kept in the shelter are missing pets, but the owner hasn't been able to find them due to the inaccessibility of information of every single shelter. The nurse decides to post on Trace in case the pet is lost. The centralized database allows pets to be reunited with their owners.

PROTOTYPING

During the prototyping phase, we were able to visualize what Trace would look like as well as narrow down what ideas we wanted to implement the following weeks. After sketching up ideas, we decided our app would have five main pages including a map and list view of the database, add pet, notifications/alerts, and lastly a settings/saved pets page.

After sketching, we were able to refine our prototype in Figma. This prototype showed key information within our pages that we wanted to implement. This prototype also allowed us to test the navigation of the app.

OVERVIEW

Trace's account database is developed utilizing Firebase authentication which allows users to sign up and keep updated on their pet. The main navigation bar includes map view, list view, add pet, updates, and account information.

The Lost Pet API is stored on the Firestore cloud and can be viewed through a map and list view. From the map view, users can click-hold to show a window with key information before clicking to navigate to the detailed pet page. Similarly, the list view shows key information in list format with an option to click to learn more.

Users can add a lost pet's name, photo, breed, map location, description, and reward value to the database. Users can also add updates to individual pets.
